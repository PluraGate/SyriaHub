## Getting Started

### Sign Up

SyriaHub uses an **invitation-only** system to maintain community quality. You'll need an invitation code from an existing member to create an account.

1. Visit `/auth/signup`
2. Enter your email, password, and invitation code
3. Verify your email address
4. Complete your profile

### User Roles

| Role | Description | Capabilities |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Member** | Community participant | Ask questions, comment, join events, follow researchers |
| **Researcher** | Verified contributor | Publish articles, share resources, organize events, invite members |
| **Moderator** | Community guardian | Review content, handle reports, manage tags |
| **Admin** | Platform manager | Full access including user management and settings |

> **Tip:** Members can apply to become Researchers through the role promotion request system.

## Creating Content

### Content Types

SyriaHub supports several content types:

| Type | Purpose | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Article** | In-depth research papers, analyses | "Reconstruction Strategies for Aleppo's Old City" |
| **Question** | Community Q&A | "What were the main causes of displacement in 2015?" |
| **Resource** | Datasets, tools, documents | "2024 Refugee Statistics CSV" |
| **Event** | Conferences, workshops, webinars | "Annual Research Symposium 2025" |

### Writing an Article

1. Click **"Write"** in the navigation bar
2. Select **"Article"** as content type
3. Enter your title (be descriptive!)
4. Write your content using Markdown
5. Add relevant **tags** (both verified and custom)
6. Choose a **license** for your work
7. Click **"Publish"** or **"Save Draft"**

### Answering Questions

1. Find a question in Insights or the Explore page
2. Scroll to the answer section
3. Write your response with citations if applicable
4. Submit your answer

## Understanding Licenses

When you publish content on SyriaHub, you choose how others can use it. Here are the available licenses:

### Creative Commons Licenses

| License | Name | What It Means |
|---------|------|---------------|
| **CC BY 4.0** | Attribution | Others can share, remix, and build upon your work (even commercially) as long as they credit you |
| **CC BY-SA 4.0** | ShareAlike | Same as CC BY, but derivatives must use the same license |
| **CC BY-NC 4.0** | NonCommercial | Others can remix and share non-commercially with credit |
| **CC0 1.0** | Public Domain | You waive all rights; anyone can use it for any purpose without credit |

### Software/Code Licenses

| License | Best For |
|---------|----------|
| **MIT** | Maximum freedom; simple, widely understood |
| **Apache 2.0** | Patent protection included; good for enterprise |

### All Rights Reserved

Choose **"All Rights Reserved"** if you want to retain full copyright and require permission for any use.

### Choosing the Right License

- **Research papers**: CC BY 4.0 (maximizes reach and citations)
- **Datasets**: CC0 or CC BY 4.0 (encourages reuse)
- **Code/Tools**: MIT or Apache 2.0
- **Sensitive content**: All Rights Reserved

> **Important:** Once published, your license choice is attached to that version. You can change it in future edits, but the original license applies to the original version.

## Gamification & Levels

SyriaHub rewards active participation with XP (experience points) and levels.

### Earning XP

| Action | XP Earned |
|--------|-----------|
| Publish an article | +50 XP |
| Answer a question | +20 XP |
| Receive an upvote | +5 XP |
| Get answer accepted | +25 XP |
| Post a comment | +5 XP |
| Complete profile | +10 XP |

### Levels & Badges

As you gain XP, you unlock new levels and earn badges for specific achievements:
- **Deep Diver**: Published 10+ articles
- **Community Pillar**: Answered 50+ questions
- **Verified Author**: Content verified by moderators
- **Event Organizer**: Hosted 5+ events

View your progress on your **Profile** page.

## Moderation & Community

### Content Review

All published content goes through moderation to ensure quality. Moderators may:
- Approve content (goes live immediately)
- Request revisions (you'll receive feedback)
- Reject content (with explanation)

### Reporting Content

If you see content that violates community guidelines:
1. Click the **"Report"** button on the post
2. Select the reason
3. Add any additional context
4. Submit

### Appeals

If your content is rejected:
1. Go to `/admin/appeals` (or check notifications)
2. Review the rejection reason
3. Submit your appeal with explanation
4. A different moderator will review

## Analytics & Insights

### Researcher Analytics

Access your analytics dashboard via your profile dropdown → **Analytics**:

- **Total Views**: How many times your content was viewed
- **Engagement**: Votes, comments, and interactions
- **Publications**: Your total published content
- **Citations**: How often others reference your work
- **Followers**: Your audience size
- **Trust Score**: Average quality rating of your work

### Trends

View charts showing:
- Views over time (7, 14, 30, or 90 days)
- Engagement trends
- Top performing content
