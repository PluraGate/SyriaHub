# Privacy Policy

> SyriaHub — Knowledge Infrastructure for Syria's Built and Social Record

*Effective: March 2026*

---

## Who This Is For

This policy describes how SyriaHub collects, uses, and protects data from researchers, contributors, and visitors. It is written in plain language because researchers working on conflict documentation deserve to understand exactly what risks they are taking when they contribute here.

**Read this policy carefully if you are:**
- A researcher based in Syria or a neighbouring country
- Someone contributing evidence about specific individuals, sites, or events
- A diaspora contributor with personal connections to documented events
- Anyone whose physical safety could be affected by the disclosure of their identity or location

---

## What We Collect

### Account information
- Email address, name, institutional affiliation (provided at registration)
- Profile information you choose to add: bio, cover image, expertise areas
- Authentication events (login, logout, password change) — retained for 90 days

### Content you submit
- Posts, traces, questions, answers, and all associated metadata
- Uploaded files (photographs, documents, audio) stored on Supabase Storage
- Version history of your edits — retained permanently as part of the knowledge record
- Citations, linked resources, and research gap contributions you create
- Expert verification documents (credential scans) — stored in restricted access storage

### Automatically collected
- IP address at time of requests — retained for 14 days in server logs, not linked to your profile
- Browser and device type via standard HTTP headers
- Session identifiers (cookies) for authentication only — no tracking cookies

### What we do NOT collect
- Location data beyond what you voluntarily include in content
- Behavioral profiles, reading history, or engagement patterns
- Cross-site tracking of any kind
- Financial or payment information (SyriaHub is free to use)

---

## Special Category: Sensitive Research Data

SyriaHub exists to document events in a conflict and post-conflict context. Some contributions may involve:
- Eyewitness testimony or oral history identifying individuals
- Geographic data that could locate people or communities
- Evidence of atrocities, displacement, or destruction

**We handle this data with the following commitments:**

1. **No commercial use.** Research data is never used for advertising, profiling, or sold to third parties.
2. **No behavioral analysis.** We do not analyse reading patterns to infer political views, affiliations, or personal circumstances.
3. **Minimal retention of operational metadata.** IP addresses in server logs are purged after 14 days. We do not correlate IPs with content authorship in any persistent store.
4. **Contributor pseudonymity.** You may use a pseudonym or institutional attribution rather than your personal name. Contact us before registering if you need to discuss pseudonymous participation.
5. **Moderation access is limited.** Moderators can see your submitted content and review correspondence sent through the platform. They cannot access private drafts, your login history, or your IP address.

## Trace Content and Archival Material

Content submitted as a Trace (archival artefacts — photographs, documents, oral testimony, handwritten materials) is treated with heightened care:

- Traces are preserved as part of the long-term record and are not deleted on request unless they contain identifiable individuals who face safety risk
- Custody chain information you provide is stored as part of the artefact's metadata
- Files uploaded as Traces are stored in Supabase Storage (EU region) and are not publicly indexed by search engines by default

If you are submitting material that contains personally identifying information about individuals in a conflict zone, consider whether:
- The person documented can be identified from the material
- Their identification could cause them harm
- You have their consent or a compelling archival justification

Moderators may request redaction of identifying details before a Trace is published.

## Data Storage and Location

SyriaHub uses Supabase (PostgreSQL database and file storage) hosted in the EU. All data at rest is encrypted. All data in transit uses TLS.

We do not use hosting infrastructure in Syria or in jurisdictions with active intelligence-sharing agreements with parties to the Syrian conflict.

## Your Rights

You have the right to:
- **Access** a copy of the data we hold about you
- **Correct** inaccurate personal information in your profile
- **Delete** your account and personal profile data

**Important limits on deletion:**
- Published content that has been cited by other researchers may be anonymised rather than deleted, to preserve the integrity of citation chains. You will be informed before this happens.
- Version history of published content is retained as part of the archival record. Individual versions may be anonymised on request.
- Trace content linked to citations or peer reviews may be retained in anonymised form.

To exercise any of these rights, contact: **privacy@syriahub.org**

---

## Research and Archive Exception

SyriaHub operates under a research and public interest exception for some data retention decisions. This is consistent with GDPR Article 89 and similar frameworks. Specifically:

- Version histories of published research are retained indefinitely because they are part of the knowledge record
- Research gaps, citations, and knowledge graph relationships are retained because removing them would damage the integrity of the collective record

We apply this exception conservatively and only where there is genuine archival value.

We use one cookie:
- `sb-auth-token`: Session authentication. Required for login. Expires when you log out or after 7 days of inactivity.

No advertising cookies. No analytics cookies. No third-party tracking.
